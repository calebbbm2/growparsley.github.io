  <main>
    <section>
      <h2>🌿 Introduction</h2>
      <p>Parsley (<em>Petroselinum crispum</em>) is a biennial herb commonly grown for its fresh, peppery leaves used in cooking and medicinal remedies. It’s an essential herb in many kitchens and gardens due to its hardiness, versatility, and nutritional benefits.</p>
    </section>

    <section>
      <h2>📌 Planting & Growing Conditions</h2>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Spacing:</strong> Plant 20 cm (8 inches) apart</li>
        <li><strong>Sunlight:</strong> Full sun to partial shade (4–6 hours daily)</li>
        <li><strong>Water:</strong> Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy</li>
        <li><strong>Soil Type:</strong> Rich, well-draining loamy soil (pH 6.0–7.0)</li>
        <li><strong>Temperature Range:</strong> 18–24°C (65–75°F)</li>
        <li><strong>Fertilizer:</strong> Apply balanced liquid fertilizer every 4–6 weeks</li>
        <li><strong>Support:</strong> Not required – parsley is a compact, self-supporting plant</li>
        <li><strong>Time to Harvest:</strong> 70–90 days from sowing</li>
      </ul>

    <section>
      <h2>🐛 Common Pests</h2>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Above ground:</strong> Aphids, spider mites, leaf miners</li>
        <li><strong>Below ground:</strong> Root-knot nematodes, carrot rust fly larvae</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
      <h2>🔍 Appearance & Flavor</h2>
      <p>Parsley has bright green foliage and can be either flat-leaf (Italian) or curly-leaf. The flavor is fresh, slightly bitter, and mildly peppery – perfect for enhancing savory dishes.</p>
    </section>

    <section>
      <h2>🍴 Uses of Parsley</h2>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Culinary:</strong> Used in sauces, soups, salads, dressings, and as a garnish</li>
        <li><strong>Medicinal:</strong> Traditionally used to support kidney function and digestion</li>
        <li><strong>Nutritional:</strong> Rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron and flavonoids</li>
        <li><strong>Companion Planting:</strong> Improves flavor when planted near tomatoes</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
      <h2>🥗 Featured Recipes</h2>
      <h3>Tabbouleh Salad</h3>
      <p><strong>Ingredients:</strong> Parsley, bulgur wheat, tomatoes, mint, lemon juice, olive oil</p>
      <p><strong>Instructions:</strong> Soak bulgur, finely chop parsley and vegetables, combine and chill before serving.</p>

      <h3>Chimichurri Sauce</h3>
      <p><strong>Ingredients:</strong> Parsley, garlic, olive oil, red wine vinegar, red pepper flakes</p>
      <p><strong>Instructions:</strong> Finely chop parsley and garlic, mix with oil and vinegar, let sit before serving over grilled meats.</p>
    </section>

    <section>
      <h2>🌟 Expert Tips for Success</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Soak seeds overnight to speed up germination (14–28 days otherwise)</li>
        <li>Harvest early in the morning for peak flavor and oil content</li>
        <li>Cut outer leaves to encourage continuous growth</li>
        <li>Keep away from dill and mint, which may compete in the garden</li>
        <li>Watch for yellowing leaves, which indicate overwatering or poor soil drainage</li>
        <li>Let parsley flower only if collecting seeds – otherwise, snip flower stalks</li>
      </ul>
